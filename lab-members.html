Current team members

  • Julius Jacobsen is a Senior Software Developer responsible for Exomiser, Phenopackets and many other tools
  • Valentina Cipriani is a Senior Lecturer in Statistical Genomics working on gene burden approaches for rare disease gene discovery and the complex genetics of age-related macular degeneration (AMD)
  • Pilar Cacheiro is a Lecturer in Computational Biology and has been leading our research interests on the IMPC project for the last 6 years
  • Letizia Vestito is a Research Fellow in Computational Biology, working on novel diagnostics and gene discovery approaches
  • Yasemin Bridges is a Bioinformatician/Data Scientist working on our Monarch Initiative project on Exomiser and the Pheval framework for benchmarking of phenotype prioritisation tools
  • Carlo Kroll is a Software Developer working on our Monarch Initiative project on improving Exomiser
  • Gabriel Marengo is a Bioinformatician/Data Scientist working on our MorPhiC project to integrate multiple data sources and visualise the results.
  • Diego Pava is a Bioinformatician/Data Scientist working on the IMPC project, linking human disease knowledge to the mouse models
  • Marta Delfino is a Bioinformatician/Data Scientist working on machine learning approaches on the IMPC data
  • Emma Magavern is a PhD student studying pharmacogenomics in ethnically diverse populations and jointly supervised by Professor Sir Mark Caulfield
  • Krishna Amim is a PhD student on the AI for drug discovery programme researching diseases of aging using a multiomics approach
